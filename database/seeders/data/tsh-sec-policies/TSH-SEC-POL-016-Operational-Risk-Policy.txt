OPERATIONAL RISK POLICY
BY PRZEMYSŁAW SZULECKI, CSO
This policy establishes TSH's framework for identifying, assessing, and managing operational risks across people, processes, technology, and third parties. Aligned to DORA Art.6, NIS2 Art.21, and ISO 27001.
TSH-SEC-POL-016  |  v1.0  |  2025-01-01  |  CSO — Przemysław Szulecki
# 1. Purpose & Scope
This policy establishes the framework for identifying, assessing, managing, and monitoring operational risks at The Software House. Operational risk is defined as the risk of loss resulting from inadequate or failed internal processes, people, systems, or external events.
Scope: all TSH business operations, including software delivery, internal IT, HR processes, supplier management, and client engagements. This policy complements the Information Security Risk Management procedure (PROC-206) with a broader operational lens.

# 3. Risk Appetite & Tolerance
## 3.1 TSH Operational Risk Appetite
TSH accepts low-to-moderate operational risk in pursuit of business growth. Zero tolerance applies to: regulatory non-compliance (GDPR, NIS2), client data loss, and safety-related failures.
# 4. Operational Risk Management Process
## 4.1 Identification
Operational risks are identified through:
Monthly operational review meetings (team leads report issues)
Post-incident reviews (every P1/P2 incident generates risk assessment)
Project retrospectives (PMs flag process failures)
Regulatory monitoring (CSO tracks NIS2, DORA, GDPR developments)
Supplier performance reviews (quarterly vendor assessments)
## 4.2 Assessment
Risks scored using a 5×5 matrix: Likelihood (1–5) × Impact (1–5) = Risk Score (1–25). See PROC-213 (Operational Risk Procedure) for full methodology.

# 5. Roles & Responsibilities
# 6. Reporting
Monthly: CSO reviews Operational Risk Register (REG-011) — update scores, treatment status
Quarterly: CSO presents Operational Risk Summary to Management Board
Annual: Full Operational Risk Assessment — all categories reviewed, register reset/updated
Ad hoc: Critical or High risks escalated immediately to Management Board

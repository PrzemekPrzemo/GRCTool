CLEAR DESK & SCREEN POLICY
BY PRZEMYSŁAW SZULECKI, CSO
Minimises risk of information exposure through unattended screens and unsecured documents. Covers screen lock enforcement (IRU/Intune), screen sharing protocol, clear desk, and shared spaces.
TSH-SEC-POL-012  |  v2.1  |  2025-01-01  |  CSO — Przemysław Szulecki
# 1. Purpose
This policy reduces the risk of unauthorised access to information through unattended screens, printed documents, and unsecured workspaces. It applies to all TSH personnel — remote workers and any office or co-working space use.
# 2. Screen Security
## 2.1 Screen Lock
Lock your screen whenever you leave your device — even briefly. Shortcut: Cmd+Ctrl+Q (macOS) or Win+L (Windows).
Automatic screen lock after 5 minutes of inactivity — enforced by IRU (macOS/iOS) and Intune (Windows). Do not disable or extend this.
Strong password or biometric (Touch ID / Face ID) required to unlock — never use no-lock or a simple PIN on work devices.
Require password immediately after screen lock — set to "immediately" in macOS System Settings → Lock Screen.
## 2.2 Before Screen Sharing
Lock or close any windows containing CONFIDENTIAL or RESTRICTED information before starting screen share.
Share only the specific window needed — not full screen — when sensitive content is visible elsewhere.
Check background before joining video calls: no whiteboards, sensitive documents, or second screens with confidential content visible.
Disable notifications during calls: macOS → Focus → Do Not Disturb. Notifications may reveal sensitive message previews.
# 3. Clear Desk
No physical documents classified CONFIDENTIAL or higher left face-up on desk when unattended.
At end of working session: all documents stored securely or face-down; nothing left visible overnight.
Printed documents: collect immediately from printer; do not leave unattended.
Documents no longer needed: cross-cut shred — do not put in general bin or recycling.
Whiteboard: photograph sensitive content, then erase before leaving the space.
# 4. Visitors & Shared Spaces
Clear all sensitive materials from view before any visitor enters your workspace.
In co-working spaces: treat every surrounding person as a potential observer — use privacy screen, position monitor away from walkways.
Never leave device unattended and unlocked in any shared or public space — this is a P3 security incident if discovered.
# 5. Compliance
Violations are reported to CSO. IT Lead performs periodic random compliance checks (quarterly). Repeated violations addressed through HR process. IRU and Intune enforce screen lock technically — any attempt to disable will be logged and flagged.

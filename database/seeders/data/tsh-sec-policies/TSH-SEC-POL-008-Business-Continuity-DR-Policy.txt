BUSINESS CONTINUITY & DR POLICY
BY PRZEMYSŁAW SZULECKI, CSO
Defines TSH's approach to business continuity and disaster recovery. RTO/RPO targets per system, key risk scenarios, backup requirements, and annual testing obligations.
TSH-SEC-POL-008  |  v2.0  |  2025-01-01  |  CSO — Przemysław Szulecki
# 1. Purpose & Scope
This policy defines TSH's approach to maintaining business continuity and recovering from disruptive events. TSH's 90% remote model and cloud-native architecture provide inherent resilience that this policy builds upon.
# 2. Recovery Objectives
# 3. Key Risks & Continuity Plans
## 3.1 Cloud Provider Outage
Primary cloud (Google/Microsoft): switch to backup communication channels (Teams if Google down; Google Meet if Teams down)
Code access: all developers maintain local clone of active branches
IRU MDM outage: device policies persist locally; defer policy changes until service restored
## 3.2 Ransomware / Destructive Attack
Immediate: isolate affected devices via IRU Remote Lock / Intune isolate; do NOT pay ransom
Restore from GitHub (code) and Google Vault (documents) — not from affected devices
Activate incident response (PROC-202); notify Management Board and CSO immediately
## 3.3 Key Person Unavailability
CSO: documented deputy; break-glass accounts with Management Board
IT Lead: two documented deputies; all IT procedures documented in Confluence
Critical knowledge: documented in Confluence — no single-person knowledge silos
# 4. Backup Requirements
# 5. Testing
Annual BCP tabletop exercise — scenario-based; CSO leads; Management Board participates
Annual DR test — restore from backup; verify RTO targets met
Quarterly backup restoration test — random sample of files/repos
Semi-annual emergency communication test — can we reach all personnel in 1 hour?
TSH's 90% remote model is itself a BCP control — no single office failure can disable the workforce. This advantage should be explicitly communicated to clients during RFP due diligence.

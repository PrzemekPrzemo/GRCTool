ACCEPTABLE USE POLICY
BY PRZEMYSŁAW SZULECKI, CSO
Defines acceptable and unacceptable use of TSH systems, devices, and data for all personnel. Covers devices (IRU/Intune), passwords, email, AI tools, and prohibited activities.
TSH-SEC-POL-004  |  v2.0  |  2025-01-01  |  CSO — Przemysław Szulecki
# 1. Purpose & Scope
This policy defines acceptable and unacceptable use of TSH systems, networks, devices, and data. It applies to all TSH employees, contractors, and third parties. Violations may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination.
# 2. Acceptable Use
## 2.1 Work Devices
Use TSH-managed devices (IRU-enrolled for Apple; Intune-enrolled for Windows) for TSH work
Keep devices up to date — critical patches deploy automatically via IRU/Intune; do not defer more than 48 hours
Lock screen when leaving device unattended — enforced automatically after 5 minutes
Do not share your device with family or other non-TSH personnel
Only install applications from the TSH approved list (POL-015); request new apps via Jira IT ticket
## 2.2 Accounts & Passwords
Use 1Password for all passwords — no other password managers, no browser password saving for work accounts
Never share credentials — each person has their own account; shared accounts are prohibited except CSO-approved exceptions
Never reuse passwords across systems
Report suspected account compromise to IT immediately
## 2.3 Internet & Email
Acceptable: work-related research, professional development, reasonable personal use that does not consume excessive resources
Email: apply the same care as written correspondence — once sent, cannot be recalled
Do not click links or open attachments in suspicious emails — report to IT (it@tsh.io)
Do not forward work emails to personal email accounts
## 2.4 Data Handling
Access only data necessary for your role (least privilege)
Store work data in approved TSH systems (Google Drive, GitHub, Confluence) — not personal cloud storage
Do not copy client RESTRICTED data to personal devices or services without CSO approval
Follow data classification labels — do not share CONFIDENTIAL data beyond its permitted audience
## 2.5 AI Tools
AI tools are permitted only per the approved list in POL-013. Never submit client source code, personal data, or CONFIDENTIAL/RESTRICTED information to any AI tool not explicitly approved by the CSO. This includes public ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude web interfaces unless approved.
# 3. Unacceptable Use
The following activities are prohibited on TSH systems and managed devices, without exception.
# 4. Monitoring
TSH monitors use of managed devices and corporate systems for security purposes. This includes endpoint telemetry via Microsoft Defender, device compliance via IRU/Intune, email scanning, and access logs. Monitoring is proportionate and focused on security threats, not general surveillance.
# 5. Reporting Violations
Report suspected violations of this policy to the CSO (cso@tsh.io) or IT Lead (it@tsh.io). Reports made in good faith will not result in retaliation. Anonymous reporting: security@tsh.io.

THIRD-PARTY VENDOR RISK POLICY
BY PRZEMYSŁAW SZULECKI, CSO
Defines how TSH classifies, onboards, and manages security risks from vendors and sub-contractors, aligned with ISO/IEC 27036-1:2021. Four-tier classification; contractual requirements including DPA and DORA provisions.
TSH-SEC-POL-006  |  v2.2  |  2026-07-13  |  CSO — Przemysław Szulecki
# 1. Purpose & Scope
This policy defines how TSH manages security risks from third-party vendors, suppliers, and sub-contractors. It is aligned with ISO/IEC 27036-1:2021 and applies to any external party with access to TSH systems, data, or client environments, or that TSH relies upon to deliver services to clients.

# 3. Vendor Onboarding
## 3.1 Due Diligence (Tier 1 & 2)
Security questionnaire completed by vendor
Review vendor's latest SOC 2 Type II report or ISO 27001 certificate
Confirm data residency (EU-only or documented cross-border transfer mechanism)
Confirm sub-processor list and any fourth-party risk
Review vendor's incident notification commitment
## 3.2 Contractual Requirements
# 4. Sub-contractor Management
Important. TSH is responsible for the security practices of all sub-contractors on client projects. "I didn't know" is not a defence under GDPR, DORA or client regulations — equivalent controls are mandatory.
Sub-contractors require PM + CSO approval before engagement
Client consent required before any sub-contractor accesses client environment or data
Sub-contractors must sign TSH NDA and security requirements schedule before access; completed REF-019 (including ABAC declaration)
Only managed, MDM-enrolled devices (no BYOD) when accessing client environments / RESTRICTED data
Access limited to what is required, equivalent to TSH minimums (MFA, device encryption, no client data on personal systems); revoked within 5 days of engagement end (PROC-210)
Sub-contractors listed in DPA Annex 3 for relevant client projects
# 5. Open Source Software (OSS)
SCA (Snyk + Dependabot) mandatory in all CI/CD pipelines — blocks critical CVEs
Licence review at project inception — GPL, AGPL, LGPL require CSO review before use in client deliverable
SBOM (SPDX 2.3 or CycloneDX 1.4+) delivered with every production release
No OSS libraries with known critical/high CVEs in production deliverables without written client acceptance
# 6. Vendor Register
IT Lead maintains REG-010 (Third-Party Register) with all Tier 1 and Tier 2 vendors. Register reviewed quarterly. DORA register information (Art. 28/30) maintained separately for applicable client engagements.
Tier 1 vendors must be reassessed annually regardless of whether any incident has occurred. Reassessment uses the same due diligence checklist as initial onboarding.

Tier 3 — Standard | Limited data access; replaceable without significant impact | Design tools, analytics tools, minor SaaS | Annual review; privacy policy review; NDA recommended
Tier 4 — Low | No sensitive data access | Office supplies, courier services | Standard contract; no specific security requirements

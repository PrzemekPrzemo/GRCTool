ANTI-BRIBERY & BUSINESS ETHICS POLICY
BY PRZEMYSŁAW SZULECKI, CSO
Zero tolerance for bribery and corruption. Prohibited conduct, gift thresholds, third-party obligations (incl. KSA/Nazaha), conflicts of interest and reporting channels.
TSH-SEC-POL-018  |  v1.0  |  2026-07-13  |  CSO — Przemysław Szulecki
# 1. Scope
TSH conducts business with integrity and in compliance with anti-corruption law. This policy covers all employees, contractors, sub-contractors and agents, regardless of jurisdiction. TSH operates under Polish and EU law and — when serving clients in the KSA — under Saudi anti-bribery law (Royal Decree M/36) and the NCA framework.

Zero tolerance. TSH permits no bribery or corruption in any form or circumstance. There are no exceptions for "facilitation payments", "customary gifts" or "competitive necessity".
# 2. Prohibited Conduct and Gift Thresholds
Prohibited: bribery of officials, commercial bribery (kickbacks/commissions), facilitation payments, improper gifts/hospitality, undisclosed conflicts of interest, falsification of records.

Gifts received/given: permissible up to the equivalent of PLN 200 / SAR 200, infrequent, creating no obligation; disclose to manager over PLN 100, to CSO over PLN 200.

Hospitality received: disclose over PLN 300 per event.

Travel/accommodation and any gifts to officials — only with prior CEO + CSO/CFO approval.
# 3. Third Parties, Conflicts of Interest and Reporting
All third parties must meet equivalent standards: due diligence under POL-006, an anti-bribery clause in contracts, ABAC declarations in REF-019.

Conflicts of interest are disclosed in writing to the manager and CSO before engagement; recusal from decisions applies.

Reporting channels (protected from retaliation): CSO (cso@tsh.io); CEO (where it concerns the CSO/senior management); anonymously (security@tsh.io); for KSA operations — Saudi Nazaha (nazaha.gov.sa).

ABAC training: onboarding (TRN-001) and an annual refresh.

Violations: serious misconduct, up to termination and referral to authorities.

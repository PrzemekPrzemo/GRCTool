SECURITY TRAINING AND AWARENESS POLICY
BY PRZEMYSŁAW SZULECKI, CSO
Mandatory security awareness programme: onboarding training, annual refresh, phishing simulations, and consequences for non-completion. Full schedule and tracking mechanics — PROC-207.
TSH-SEC-POL-019  |  v1.0  |  2026-07-14  |  CSO — Przemysław Szulecki
# 1. Purpose & Scope
This policy sets the minimum security awareness level required of everyone with access to TSH systems or data — employee, contractor, or associate — regardless of role. Training is a condition of client-project access, not optional.
# 2. Training Programme
Onboarding training (Module 1): all new starters, within 5 days of start, before client-project access (PROC-201).
Annual refresh: all personnel, once per year (Q4).
Secure coding (OWASP): developers, annually.
Security Champions: one designated person per team, quarterly.
Board training: CSO and Management Board, annually.
# 3. Phishing Simulation and Non-Completion Consequences
Phishing simulation run quarterly for everyone; target click rate ≤5%.
Clicking a simulation is not punished — it triggers mandatory 5-day microtraining.
Non-completion of mandatory training within 14 days: escalation to line manager. After 30 days: TSH system access restricted.
Results and completion rates reported quarterly to CSO; training records retained 3 years (GDPR accountability + ISO 27001 evidence).

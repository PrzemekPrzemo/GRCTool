# 1. Purpose & Scope
This policy defines the framework for responding to information security incidents at TSH. It establishes classification, roles, notification obligations, and response principles. See PROC-202 for step-by-step procedure, PROC-217 for communication templates, and PROC-218 for scenario-specific playbooks.

# 3. Response Principles
Contain first, investigate second — stop the spread before determining root cause
Preserve evidence — use IRU Lock (not Wipe) on Apple devices during active investigation
Need-to-know communications — incident details restricted to response team
Document everything in real time — update REG-009 throughout
Never pay ransom without Management Board + legal counsel decision
# 4. Roles
# 5. Notification Obligations
When in doubt about notification — notify. The cost of over-notifying is far lower than the cost of late notification. Use PROC-217 templates — they are pre-approved.
# 6. Communication Templates & Playbooks
PROC-217 (Incident Communication Templates) contains six pre-approved, ready-to-send templates covering all notification scenarios. Use these during incidents — they are pre-approved and require only [BRACKET] completion.
PROC-218 (Incident Response Playbooks) provides step-by-step guides for three high-probability scenarios: ransomware/malware, GitHub credential leak, and phishing/account takeover.
# 7. Post-Incident Review
P1 incidents: mandatory post-incident report within 2 weeks; presented to Management Board
P2 incidents: post-incident review within 2 weeks; CSO reviews with IT Lead
All P1/P2: root cause analysis; lessons learned fed into training (PROC-207) and policy updates
All incidents: logged in REG-009; retained 3 years

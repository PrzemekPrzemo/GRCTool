CHANGE MANAGEMENT POLICY
BY PRZEMYSŁAW SZULECKI, CSO
Control of changes to systems, infrastructure, and code: categorisation, approval, testing, documentation, and review.
TSH-SEC-POL-016  |  v1.0  |  2026-07-14  |  CSO — Przemysław Szulecki
# 1. Purpose & Scope
This policy governs how changes to TSH systems, infrastructure, and code are proposed, approved, tested, and documented, to prevent unplanned outages, security regressions, and unauthorised modifications. Applies to IT Lead, Tech Lead, and all engineering teams.
# 2. Change Categories
Standard (pre-approved): routine, documented changes. No additional approval required.
Normal: reviewed by IT Lead / Tech Lead. Logged in REG-006.
Emergency: approved by CSO before deployment, or immediately after if deployment cannot wait. Post-deployment review within 48 hours.
# 3. Requirements for Every Change
Change description and risk assessment (security impact).
Rollback plan.
Testing in a lower environment before production deployment (exception: Emergency changes, with CSO consent).
Documentation in REG-006; post-deployment review for Normal and Emergency changes.

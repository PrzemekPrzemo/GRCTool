# 3. Change Assessment Process
## Step 1: Detect (CSO, within 5 business days of publication)
New regulation or significant amendment identified via monitoring source
CSO creates Jira ticket: IT-SECURITY → Regulatory Change → brief summary + source link
Initial triage: does this affect TSH directly, or only through client obligations?
## Step 2: Impact Assessment (CSO + relevant owner, within 14 days)
## Step 3: Update & Test (owner, within 30 days of in-force date)
Update affected documents — version bump, change log entry
Notify affected personnel (email from CSO for material changes; all-hands for major regulation)
Update REG-014 (Compliance Obligation Register) with new/changed obligation
Update REG-015 (Compliance Calendar) with any new recurring compliance tasks
Close Jira ticket with links to all updated documents
## Step 4: Management Board Notification
Material changes (new regulation, significant fine risk): notify Management Board at next quarterly meeting
Critical changes (regulation in force within 30 days with significant compliance gap): immediate notification
# 4. DORA Delegated Acts — Special Track
DORA Level 2 (RTS/ITS) is published in batches by ESAs. Each new batch requires:
Impact assessment within 14 days of official publication in EU Official Journal
Update CLT-403 (DPA) and CLT-406 (Operational Risk answers) where contractual provisions change
Client notification if existing contracts are affected (per CLT-404 review)
DORA RTS on ICT third-party risk (Art. 28–30) affects TSH most directly. CSO tracks DORA Level 2 calendar at eba.europa.eu/regulation-and-policy/digital-operational-resilience

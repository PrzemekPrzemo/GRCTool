OPERATIONAL RISK PROCEDURE
BY PRZEMYSŁAW SZULECKI, CSO
Step-by-step procedure for conducting Operational Risk Assessments at TSH. Covers identification, scoring (5×5 matrix), treatment planning, and register maintenance. Feeds into REG-011.
TSH-SEC-PROC-213  |  v1.0  |  2025-01-01  |  CSO — Przemysław Szulecki
# 1. Purpose
This procedure defines the step-by-step process for conducting Operational Risk Assessments at TSH. It operationalises the Operational Risk Policy (POL-016) and feeds results into the Operational Risk Register (REG-011).
# 2. When to Conduct an Operational Risk Assessment
# 3. Step-by-Step Assessment Process
## Step 1: Scope Definition
Define what is being assessed: business unit, process, project, or system. Document in REG-011 header row for the assessment cycle.
Who is involved: CSO (mandatory), relevant Team Lead, IT Lead (for technology risks)
Duration: 2-hour workshop for project-level; half-day for annual full assessment
Inputs: previous risk register, recent incidents, post-mortems, supplier reports
## Step 2: Risk Identification
Use structured brainstorming across all 8 operational risk categories (see POL-016 Section 2). For each category, ask: 'What could go wrong? What are we dependent on? Where do we have single points of failure?'
Document each identified risk in REG-011 with: Category, Description, Potential Cause, Potential Impact
Minimum 2 risks per category for full annual assessment
## Step 3: Risk Scoring
Score each risk using the 5×5 likelihood × impact matrix:
Risk Score = Likelihood × Impact. Record in REG-011: Inherent Score (before controls), Current Controls, Residual Score (after controls).
## Step 4: Treatment Planning
For each risk above score 5 (Medium), document a treatment plan:
Treatment type: Avoid / Mitigate / Transfer / Accept
Specific action: what will be done, by when, by whom
Target residual score after treatment
Review date (when to reassess effectiveness)
## Step 5: Register Update & Sign-off
Update REG-011 with all identified risks, scores, and treatment plans
CSO reviews and signs off — escalates Critical/High to Management Board
Share summary with Management Board at next quarterly review
Schedule treatment plan follow-up dates in JIRA
# 4. Operational Risk Register (REG-011) — Column Guide
# 5. Client-Facing Operational Risk Disclosure
When responding to client due diligence questionnaires about operational risk, refer to CLT-406 (Operational Risk — Client Answers). The following standard positions apply:
TSH does not disclose specific risk scores or individual risk register entries to clients
TSH provides a summary of operational risk governance framework and key controls
Material operational risks affecting a specific client engagement are disclosed under NDA
DORA-required ICT operational risk information is available per Art. 28/30 for regulated clients

Trigger | Type | Frequency | Owner
Scheduled annual review | Full assessment — all risk categories | Annual (Q4) | CSO
New major client engagement (>3 months) | Project operational risk assessment | At project kick-off | PM + CSO
New supplier / sub-contractor | Third-party operational risk | Before onboarding | IT Lead + CSO
Post-incident (P1 or P2) | Incident-triggered risk assessment | Within 2 weeks of incident close | CSO
Regulatory change (NIS2, DORA, GDPR) | Compliance-triggered assessment | Within 30 days of change | CSO
Significant technology change (new platform) | Technology risk assessment | Before go-live | IT Lead + CSO

[TABLE]
Score | Likelihood | Impact
1 | Rare — less than once in 5 years | Negligible — minor process disruption, no client impact
2 | Unlikely — once in 2–5 years | Minor — internal disruption, no external impact
3 | Possible — once per year | Moderate — client notices, SLA at risk
4 | Likely — several times per year | Significant — client SLA breach, potential data exposure
5 | Almost certain — monthly or more | Severe — data breach, regulatory action, contract termination
[/TABLE]

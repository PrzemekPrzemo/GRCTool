SECURITY AUDIT & REVIEW PROCEDURE
BY CSO
ISO 27001 Cl. 9.2/9.3-aligned internal audit programme. Annual ISMS management review, technical configuration audits (including IRU/Apple MDM quarterly check), quarterly access reviews.
TSH-SEC-PROC-212  |  v1.0  |  2025-01-01  |  CSO — Przemysław Szulecki
# 1. Purpose
This procedure defines how TSH conducts internal security audits and management reviews of the ISMS. Required by ISO 27001 Clause 9.2 (Internal Audit) and Clause 9.3 (Management Review).
# 2. Audit Programme
# 3. Annual ISMS Management Review Agenda
Status of actions from previous review
ISMS performance: incidents logged vs closed (REG-009); risk treatment status (REG-001 & REG-011)
Audit findings and corrective actions
Security awareness training completion rates
Changes affecting the ISMS: regulatory, technology, business
Opportunities for improvement
Decisions: policy updates needed; resource requirements; risk appetite review
# 4. Technical Configuration Audit — IRU (Apple MDM)
IT Lead reviews quarterly — check all of the following in IRU admin console (tsh.iru.io):
Blueprint: are all required profiles still applied? Any profile conflicts?
Devices: any non-enrolled Macs or iPhones? Any devices with failed policy apply?
Compliance: any devices showing non-compliant (outdated OS, disabled FileVault, no MDE)?
LAPS: are local admin passwords rotating? Any failures?
Audit log: any unexpected admin actions (wipes, policy changes) in last 90 days?
# 5. Audit Findings & Corrective Actions
All findings documented in Jira: IT-SECURITY → Internal Audit Finding
Critical findings: resolve within 7 days
Major findings: resolve within 30 days
Minor findings: resolve within 90 days
Findings summary reported at Management Review
Internal audits are not a bureaucratic exercise — they are how TSH finds and fixes problems before clients, regulators, or attackers do. CSO treats audit findings with the same priority as security incidents.

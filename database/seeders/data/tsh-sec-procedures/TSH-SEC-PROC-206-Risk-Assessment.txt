RISK ASSESSMENT PROCEDURE
BY CSO
ISO 27001 Cl. 6.1-aligned risk assessment methodology. 5×5 likelihood/impact matrix, treatment thresholds, and register update process. Annual plus triggered assessments.
TSH-SEC-PROC-206  |  v2.0  |  2025-01-01  |  CSO — Przemysław Szulecki
# 1. Purpose
This procedure defines the methodology for conducting TSH Information Security Risk Assessments (ISO 27001 Cl. 6.1). Results feed into REG-001 (Risk Register). For operational risk see PROC-213.
# 2. When to Assess
Annual: scheduled Q4 — full ISMS risk assessment
New major client engagement — project-specific risk assessment
Significant technology change — new platform, major architecture change
Post-incident: P1/P2 incident triggers risk reassessment of affected area
Regulatory change: NIS2, DORA, GDPR update triggers compliance gap assessment
# 3. Assessment Process
## Step 1: Asset Identification
Reference REG-002 (Asset Inventory). Categorise: Data assets, Systems, People, Processes. Ensure new assets added since last assessment are included.
## Step 2: Threat & Vulnerability Identification
For each asset: identify relevant threats (external attacker, insider, system failure, natural event) and vulnerabilities (missing control, configuration weakness, people gap).
## Step 3: Risk Scoring (5×5 Matrix)
Inherent Score = Likelihood × Impact. Record existing controls. Residual Score = Likelihood (with controls) × Impact (with controls).
## Step 4: Treatment
Score 1–5: Accept (with monitoring)
Score 6–11: Mitigate — document treatment plan with owner and deadline
Score 12–19: Mitigate or Transfer — escalate to Management Board
Score 20–25: Critical — immediate Management Board decision required
## Step 5: Register & Report
Update REG-001 (Risk Register) with all risks, scores, treatments
CSO presents summary to Management Board at annual management review
Treatment plans tracked as Jira tickets with quarterly review

[TABLE]
Score | Likelihood | Impact
1 | Rare — <once in 5 years | Negligible — no operational impact
2 | Unlikely — once in 2–5 years | Minor — brief disruption, self-remediated
3 | Possible — once per year | Moderate — client impact, SLA at risk
4 | Likely — several times/year | Significant — SLA breach, data exposure risk
5 | Almost Certain — monthly+ | Severe — data breach, regulatory action, contract loss
[/TABLE]

EMPLOYEE OFFBOARDING PROCEDURE
BY HR / IT LEAD / CSO
Complete security offboarding checklist for all departing TSH employees and contractors. Covers identity revocation, IRU/Intune device management, credential cleanup, and final sign-off. Applies to both voluntary and involuntary departures.
TSH-SEC-PROC-211  |  v1.0  |  2025-01-01  |  CSO — Przemysław Szulecki
This checklist must be completed for EVERY departing employee or contractor — regardless of whether the departure is voluntary or involuntary. IT Lead owns execution; CSO signs off.
# 1. Purpose & Scope
This procedure defines the complete security offboarding process for all TSH employees and contractors. It ensures that departing personnel do not retain access to TSH or client systems, data, or assets after their engagement ends.
Related documents: PROC-201 (User Lifecycle Management), PROC-210 (Supplier/Client Project Offboarding), POL-003 (Access Control Policy), POL-015 (Approved Applications Policy).
# 2. Offboarding Types & Timelines
For involuntary terminations: IT Lead MUST be notified simultaneously with the termination meeting — not after. Access revocation begins during the meeting. Do not wait.

# 7. Phase 5: Final Sign-off & Documentation
Offboarding is not complete until CSO has signed off the Jira ticket. Partial offboarding (e.g. only email disabled) is not acceptable. Every item must be ticked before closure.
# 8. Emergency Offboarding (Involuntary / Security Incident)
## 8.1 Procedure — Execute Simultaneously
HR initiates termination meeting
IT Lead (notified simultaneously): disable Entra ID account IMMEDIATELY
IT Lead: trigger IRU Remote Wipe on all company Apple devices (do not wait for return)
IT Lead: trigger Intune Wipe on Windows device
IT Lead: revoke GitHub, Slack, Google sessions within 15 minutes
CSO: review any recent access logs — check for data exfiltration indicators
Complete full Phase 2-5 checklist within 4 hours
If there are grounds to suspect data exfiltration: do NOT wipe device immediately. Preserve forensic evidence first. Consult CSO and legal counsel before wiping. IRU Lock (not Wipe) may be used to prevent use while preserving evidence.

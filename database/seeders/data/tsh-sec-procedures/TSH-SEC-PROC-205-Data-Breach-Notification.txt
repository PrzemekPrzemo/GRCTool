# 1. Purpose
This procedure defines TSH's actions in the event of a personal data breach. It operationalises POL-009 (Privacy Policy) and POL-007 (Incident Response Policy). See also PROC-202 for full incident response steps.
# 2. What Constitutes a Breach
A personal data breach is any accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorised disclosure of, or access to, personal data (GDPR Art. 4(12)). Examples:
Laptop containing client personal data lost or stolen (even if encrypted — notify unless encryption confirmed)
Email with personal data sent to wrong recipient
Database of personal data exposed via misconfigured cloud storage
Ransomware encrypting systems containing personal data
Unauthorised access to personal data by TSH employee or sub-contractor

# 4. UODO Notification Content (GDPR Art. 33(3))
Nature of the breach (categories and approximate number of data subjects; records affected)
Name and contact details of DPO/contact point (CSO: cso@tsh.io)
Likely consequences of the breach
Measures taken or proposed to address the breach and mitigate effects
# 5. When NOT to Notify UODO
Notification to UODO is NOT required if the breach is unlikely to result in a risk to the rights and freedoms of natural persons. Low-risk examples:
Encrypted device lost (AES-256, FileVault confirmed enabled) — verify via IRU before deciding
Internal email sent between two TSH employees with no external party exposure
When in doubt — notify UODO. Late notification carries higher regulatory risk than unnecessary notification. Document the decision either way.
